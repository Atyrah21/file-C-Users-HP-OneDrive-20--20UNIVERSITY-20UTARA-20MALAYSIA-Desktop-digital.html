🎼 Traditional Gamelan Music
Enjoy the calming traditional Gamelan melody while exploring Malaysia's intangible heritage.
🧵 Batik Kelantan
Batik Kelantan is a traditional textile art known for its bright colours and floral motifs, created using canting (fine wax drawing) and block stamping techniques.

📍 Origin: Kelantan, East Coast of Malaysia
🎯 Purpose: Worn during cultural ceremonies, daily life, and official events
🎨 Technique: Canting (wax drawing) and block stamping using natural dyes
💡 Interesting Fact: Each pattern reflects traditional values, beliefs, and the identity of the Kelantanese community.
🎏 Wau Bulan
A traditional kite from Kelantan, known for its crescent moon shape and unique humming sound made by its bow when flown in strong winds.

📍 Origin: Kelantan, Malaysia
🎯 Purpose: Flown after harvest season during festivals or kite competitions. It also represents peace and gratitude in Malay culture.
💡 Interesting Fact: The Wau Bulan appears on Malaysia's 50 cent coin and MAS airline logo as a national cultural icon.
🎭 Mak Yong
A traditional Malay dance-drama that combines acting, singing, dancing, and ritual elements. It is a form of storytelling theatre rooted in oral tradition.

📍 Origin: Patani (Southern Thailand) and popularized in Kelantan, Malaysia
🎯 Purpose: Performed as entertainment, healing rituals, and spiritual storytelling with strong female roles.
🏆 UNESCO Status: Recognized as Intangible Cultural Heritage in Need of Urgent Safeguarding (2005)
💡 Interesting Fact: Traditionally, the performers were all women—even playing male roles!
🥋 Silat Melayu
Silat is a traditional Malay martial art that blends combat techniques, dance, and spiritual practices. It symbolizes strength, discipline, and cultural pride.

📍 Origin: Practiced across Malaysia, Indonesia, and Southern Thailand
🎯 Purpose: Used for self-defense, cultural performances, and ceremonial events like weddings and royal parades
🌀 Style: Combines hand-to-hand combat, weapon use (keris, tongkat), and graceful movements
💡 Interesting Fact: Silat is often performed with live music using traditional instruments like the gendang and serunai.
🕯️ Wayang Kulit
A traditional shadow puppet theatre using intricately carved leather puppets and narrated stories from folklore and epics, accompanied by live music.

📍 Origin: Kelantan, Malaysia (also in Patani, Thailand & Indonesia)
🎯 Purpose: Used for storytelling, spiritual rituals, and entertainment
🎶 Instruments: Accompanied by gong, serunai, and gendang
💡 Interesting Fact: The main storyteller, known as the Tok Dalang, voices all characters and is considered both an artist and a spiritual leader.
🎶 Dikir Barat
A traditional Malay musical form combining singing, choral chanting, and rhythmic clapping, popular in Kelantan and Terengganu.

📍 Origin: Kelantan, Malaysia (also performed in Terengganu)
🎯 Purpose: Performed during weddings, social gatherings, and festivals to foster community spirit
🎼 Style: Led by a “tok juara” (lead singer), group responds in chorus with fast-paced clapping and footwork
💡 Interesting Fact: Performances can involve improvisation, satire, and humour—often commenting on daily life or social issues.
